About Kirsten Rauffer

I've always loved the place where code + art meet. It's the closest thing to creating magic; the ability to turn a person's wildest imaginations into practical realities. In my work, I aim to create delightful experiences for all users. I advocate for accessible and inclusive design. Originally trained as an Electrical Engineer, I weave practicality with aesthetics to create end-to-end applications that users enjoy interacting with.

I'm currently a User Interface Engineer at LinkedIn, where I was based in Silicon Valley in Northern California (USA). Currently, I'm lucky enough to be working remotely while I cozy up in the Windy City of Chicago (USA). I've previously worked at Microsoft in Copenhagen (Denmark) where I explored Scandinavia, kayaked the fjords, and learned the meaning of hygge.

In addition to my expertise in web development and design, I've worked on mobile application development and design during my days as an expat; projects have ranged from published applications to proof-of-concept applications fueling Ph.D research. I've traveled for projects in cities around the globe, my farthest travel for a project being to Saint Petersburg (Russia). When I wasn't working, I was getting lost in the Hermitage.

While I can't say that I'm an interesting person, I can say that I am an interested person. Curious, and always learning. I have a love for photography, pottery, wood working, and lately, creating custom key caps. I've dabbled as a luthier. I scuba dive, trek when I can, and try to satisfy the insatiable travel bug as often as possible.

Feel free to poke around in my little corner of the World Wide Web. If you're interested in working together, reach out. This Jersey girl will get it done.
